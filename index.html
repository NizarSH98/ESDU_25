ESDU champions community-led development, resilient food systems, and inclusive growth— bridging academia and the field across Lebanon and the region.

Mission, Vision, Core Values

Grounded in research. Powered by communities. Focused on lasting change.

Mission

Harness education, research, and development to advance sustainable and resilient food systems, serving and empowering marginalized and rural communities through research, training, and outreach.

Vision

A world where communities and ecosystems thrive together — a network of self‑generative eco‑communities built on climate‑smart agriculture, circular economy, and local food systems.

ESDU at Work

What is ESDU? Explore how research translates to real-world impact.

Education & Training

Capacity building through CEC courses, school programs, and KariaNet online platform. Training 5,000+ development actors with focus on agricultural management, evaluation practices, and participatory development across MENA region.

R&D Projects

Applied research bridging field and lab: circular economy, AI/digital literacy, climate‑smart agriculture. Current projects include SUROFLY, Med4Waste, CORUSUS, and innovative feed technologies for sustainable farming.

Community Outreach

People‑centered approaches empowering smallholder farmers, women-led MSMEs, and youth. Supporting 275+ farmers, 80+ cooperatives, and vulnerable communities through capacity building and market linkages.

Communication & Dissemination

Knowledge management through conferences, peer‑reviewed publications, FAO guidelines, and regional networks (EvalMENA, KariaNet, RUAF MENA). Hosting landmark workshops with parliamentarians and ministers.

Strategic Partnerships

Collaborating with IDRC, IFAD, FAO, WFP, AFESD, UNDP, and regional institutions. Sister organizations include Food Heritage Foundation, Ardı Ardak, Eco‑Khalleh, and Animal Encounter for transformative change.

Multistakeholder Initiatives

Flagship programs: Keepers of the Land, Urban Oasis engagement center, DAWWERA circular economy network, ITHMAR heritage initiative, and 10+ living labs demonstrating sustainable solutions.

Keepers of the Land

A Keeper of the Land is anyone dedicated to preserving and protecting our heritage, agriculture, and natural resources for future generations. ESDU's flagship initiative connects small producers to markets, supports land stewards with resources and recognition, and sustains rural livelihoods through the Keepers of the Land Research Fund launched in 2015.

Strategic Goals 2025–2030

Five interconnected pillars guiding ESDU's next chapter.

Innovative ESDU

Circular economy, AI/digitalization, design for sustainability, food systems transformation, and heritage preservation through cutting-edge research and development projects.

Bright ESDU

Education for sustainable food systems through online platforms, CEC courses, school programs, increased scholarships, and expanded student recruitment across the region.

Engaged ESDU

Community engagement, strategic partnerships, network approaches, and the Urban Oasis as a digital technology showcase for transformative change.

Smart ESDU

AI & digital literacy, collaborative innovation, online presence, digital operations, and regional/international partnerships driving the digital age transition.

Sustainable ESDU

Team retention, economic growth, institutional support, innovative fundraising, diversified income streams, and streamlined collaboration with sister organizations.
